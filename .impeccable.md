# Design Context — openhistorymap.github.io

This file guides design work in this repo. The institutional site for Open History Map (the Italian non-profit) — *not* the map app or the index app on sister subdomains.

## Users

**Primary**: academic and digital-humanities peers — archaeologists, GIS researchers, DH practitioners, museum technologists. They arrive looking for proof that OHM is a serious technical and scholarly partner: what's been presented at which conferences, what's been published, who runs it, what stack it's built on.

They are skim-readers but careful skim-readers. They will judge OHM by whether the institutional site behaves like a research outfit or like a startup landing page. The single worst outcome is "looks like a SaaS hero with vague claims."

Secondary audiences (curious public, funders) are downstream of getting the scholarly register right.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **surveyed, indexed, marginalia**.

Voice: precise, measured, slightly under-sold. Made by people who measure things and cite their sources. The org has a real heritage — KAINUA in the logo refers to the Etruscan city at Marzabotto, near Bologna — and that historical depth should be felt, not decorated.

Emotional goal: the visitor should think "these are the right people to trust with this data" — confidence, not delight.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Cartographic / technical.** Treat each page as a survey sheet, not a brochure. Use:

- An explicit, visible grid the reader can sense.
- Marginalia: dates, coordinates, slugs, paper IDs, conference codes set in the meta register *next to* content, not buried in subheads.
- Tick rules and coordinate-style labels in place of cards-with-shadows.
- Generous margins and a calm body measure (~65ch).
- Drop-cap or shoulder-note moments at section openings — the page should feel composed, not flowed.

**Theme**: build both, default to **light**, respect the OS setting via `light-dark()`.

**Anti-references** (all explicitly rejected by the user):
- Generic SaaS landing — gradient hero, three-icon feature row, blue CTA.
- Stock academic site — Bootstrap-era navy header, Times body, dense unstyled lists.
- Bootcamp / dev-tool — dark mode + neon + glow + monospaced everything as a vibe.
- The current ananke theme — Tachyons utility skin, cover image + black overlay, bland recent-posts list. **What's there now is the baseline to leave behind, not iterate on.**

## Typography

Three families, each with a clear job. None are on the impeccable reflex-reject list and none are my training-data defaults:

- **Display — Petrona** (Omnibus Type, OFL). Variable humanist serif with real weight axis, designed for editorial use. Carries the heritage weight that KAINUA / Etruscan / Bologna implies without being a generic "elegant serif." Used for page titles, section openings, and the occasional shoulder-note drop cap. **v1 substitute** for the originally-specced Bagnard (Velvetyne) — Bagnard isn't on a CORS-friendly CDN, so we ship Petrona via Google Fonts for now and swap to self-hosted Bagnard once font hosting is in place.
- **Body — Hanken Grotesk** (Hanken Design Co., OFL). Modern grotesque with strong x-height and excellent screen legibility. Carries the *modern measurement* half of the brand story — archaeology re-presented as cartography. Used for all sustained reading.
- **Meta — PT Mono** (ParaType, OFL). Square, ledger-like. Used for the cartographic register: dates, coordinates, conference IDs, paper handles, file slugs, citations, nav active-state markers. Never used for body text or as decoration.

Type scale: fluid `clamp()` for display headings, fixed `rem` ladder for body and UI. Minimum 1.25 ratio between steps. Five steps, not eight.

## Color

Tight, brand-specific palette. All values in OKLCH; neutrals tinted toward the brand hue (warm paper).

| Token | Light | Dark | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| `--paper` | `oklch(0.97 0.008 80)` | `oklch(0.18 0.012 250)` | Page surface |
| `--ink` | `oklch(0.22 0.015 80)` | `oklch(0.92 0.010 80)` | Body text |
| `--ink-muted` | `oklch(0.45 0.012 80)` | `oklch(0.70 0.010 80)` | Secondary text, metadata |
| `--rule` | `oklch(0.85 0.010 80)` | `oklch(0.32 0.012 250)` | Hairlines, tick rules, dividers |
| `--terracotta` | `oklch(0.55 0.15 35)` | `oklch(0.65 0.13 35)` | Sole accent — links, current-page marker, focus rings |
| `--patina` | `oklch(0.50 0.08 220)` | `oklch(0.62 0.07 220)` | Rare second accent — only for secondary metadata bands or chart accents |

**Why these**: the palette mimics fired-clay (terracotta is the Etruscan pottery slip color) over warm map paper, with oxidized-bronze patina as the rare second voice. It is recognizably *of this org* — not a generic "warm" palette.

**60-30-10**: paper dominates, ink + ink-muted carry text and structure, terracotta appears only where it actually means something (active link, current section, focused control). Patina is a once-or-twice-per-page maximum.

## Design Principles

1. **The page is a survey sheet, not a brochure.** Visible grid, marginalia, tick rules, coordinate labels. The chrome is part of the content.
2. **Metadata is first-class.** Dates, venues, coauthors, file formats are surfaced in the meta register (PT Mono) alongside the body — not buried in subheads or hidden in tooltips.
3. **Earn every word.** No "we believe in X," no triple-icon feature rows, no marketing intro that restates the page title.
4. **Heritage in the type, modernity in the structure.** Bagnard supplies historical weight; Hanken Grotesk + a hard 4pt grid keep it from feeling antique.
5. **Restraint with the accent.** Terracotta is rare on purpose. If it appears more than ~5 times on a page, it has lost its meaning.

## Constraints

- **Hugo** (extended). All work must be expressible as Hugo templates + static CSS. No build step beyond `hugo --minify`.
- **Replacing ananke**, not iterating on it. New templates live under `layouts/` and shadow the theme; CSS is shipped as a single asset (or pipelined via Hugo Pipes).
- **Self-hosted webfonts** (the org is privacy-conscious — Matomo, no GA on the partial). No Google Fonts CDN; download Bagnard / Hanken Grotesk / PT Mono and serve from `static/fonts/` with `font-display: swap`.
- **WCAG 2.1 AA** as a floor. All text combinations above must hit ≥4.5:1; verify before shipping.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` and `prefers-color-scheme` (the latter via `light-dark()`).
